## Finnish Rating System (SIL - Suomen Ilmailuliitto)

Primary system for Foxer app. Source: varjoliitokoulu.fi

| Level | Finnish Name | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| PP1 | Peruskurssi osa 1 | Ground handling, basic theory (10h), 5 supervised low flights |
| PP2 | Peruskurssi osa 2 | Advanced theory (15h), 40 flights, independent flying basics |
| PP3 | Jatkokurssi | Advanced flying, thermal techniques, XC |
| PP2MO | Moottorivarjoliito | Motorized paragliding add-on (requires PP2) |

### PP1 + PP2 Combined Course (Varjoliitokoulu)
- **Price**: €2,000 (equipment included)
- **Location**: Kellokoski, Tuusula + various southern Finland sites
- **Method**: Tow-launch from flat terrain
- **Requirements**: Age 15-69, weight 45-135 kg, SIL membership
- **Instructor**: Jekki Kettu (300+ pilots trained)

### PP1 Phase
- 10 hours theory
- Ground handling (inflate, kite, control)
- 5 low training flights
- Basic aerodynamics and safety

### PP2 Phase
- 15 hours theory
- 40 flights at various sites
- Advanced aerodynamics and meteorology
- Independent flight skills
- Site assessment basics

### PP2MO (Motorized) — €750
- Two modules: motor theory + practical
- 7 motorized flights
- Engine maintenance, safe takeoff/landing, emergency procedures

## International Reference: USHPA Rating Levels

| Level | Rating | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| P1 | Beginner | Can take off, fly straight, land. Flies under direct instructor supervision only. |
| P2 | Novice | Turns, maneuvering, landing estimation. Has flown from higher ground under supervision. Confident handling in stronger winds. |
| P3 | Intermediate | Can fly independently at familiar sites. Thermal flying basics, cross-country introduction. |
| P4 | Advanced | Flies varied conditions and sites. Advanced thermal techniques, XC flying, turbulence handling. |
| P5 | Master | Highest rating. Expert-level skills across all conditions. |

## Training Curriculum by Level

### P1 - Beginner (2 days typical)
- Ground handling (inflating, kiting, controlling on ground)
- Basic ground school (aerodynamics, equipment, safety)
- ~20 training hill flights
- Forward launch technique
- Straight flight and gentle turns
- Flare landing

### P2 - Novice (4 days / ~160 hours curriculum)
- Launching from higher sites
- S-turns, 360-degree turns
- Speed control (using brakes and speedbar)
- Basic soaring (ridge lift)
- Introduction to thermal flying
- Basic meteorology (wind, clouds, stability)
- Site analysis and flight planning
- Emergency procedures (reserve deployment theory)

### P3 - Intermediate
- Independent site assessment
- Thermal centering and climbing
- Cross-country flight planning
- Advanced meteorology (thermals, inversions, convergence)
- Active flying in turbulence
- Asymmetric and frontal collapse recovery
- Spiral dives and B-line stalls
- Ridge soaring techniques

### P4 - Advanced
- Flying in strong conditions
- Long XC flights
- Competition flying basics
- Advanced collapse recovery at altitude (SIV course)
- Mountain flying (valley winds, rotors)
- Airspace rules and regulations
- Instrument use (vario, GPS, flight computers)

## Pre-Flight Safety Checklist

### Equipment Check
1. **Wing**: Spread and inspect for tears, UV damage, porosity
2. **Lines**: Check for tangles, knots, wear, proper tension
3. **Risers**: Inspect maillons, carabiners, speed bar connections
4. **Harness**: Check straps, buckles, stitching, load-bearing areas
5. **Reserve parachute**: Pins secure, handle accessible, repack date current
6. **Helmet**: Secure fit, no cracks
7. **Instruments**: Vario charged, GPS working

### Pre-Launch Check (5-point)
1. **Helmet** — fastened
2. **Harness** — leg straps, chest strap, all buckles locked
3. **Risers** — connected, no twists
4. **Lines** — clear, no tangles in canopy
5. **Wind** — direction and strength appropriate for site and skill level

## Weather Conditions

### Safe Conditions for Beginners
- **Wind speed**: 8-20 km/h (5-12 mph), steady direction
- **Thermals**: Avoid strong thermal days; stable/calm air is best
- **Visibility**: Good (no fog, heavy cloud, or precipitation)
- **Best time**: Early morning or late afternoon (less thermal activity)
- **Winter**: Often ideal — stable atmosphere, fewer thermals

### Danger Signs
- Gusty or variable wind (>20 km/h for beginners)
- Cumulonimbus clouds (thunderstorm risk)
- Rotor turbulence (downwind of ridges)
- Strong thermals with dust devils visible
- Rapidly changing conditions

### Wind Speed Reference
| Condition | km/h | mph | Suitability |
|-----------|------|-----|-------------|
| Calm | 0-5 | 0-3 | Light wind launch practice |
| Light | 5-15 | 3-9 | Ideal for beginners |
| Moderate | 15-25 | 9-15 | Intermediate+ |
| Strong | 25-35 | 15-22 | Advanced only |
| Very strong | 35+ | 22+ | Do not fly |

## Flight Sites

### Finland
Most flying is **Nordic ground towing** (flat terrain, few hills).

Notable sites:
- **Ylläs Fjell** (Lapland) — best in spring, weeks 14-17
- **Himos** — ski resort, 200m height, good training hill, toplanding possible
- **Nummenpää / Hyvinkää** — tandem flights
- **Kärkölä** — alternative site depending on wind
- **Saana Mountain** — northern Lapland
- **Kiikala** — towing site
- **Kauhajoki** — towing site
- **Leivonmäki** — central Finland

### Top European Sites
- **Annecy, France** — "European capital of paragliding", lake + mountains
- **Interlaken, Switzerland** — Alpine flying, Jungfrau region
- **Dolomites, Italy** — dramatic mountain flying
- **Chamonix, France** — Mont Blanc area, advanced terrain
- **Ölüdeniz, Turkey** — famous coastal soaring
- **Slovenia** — accessible, good conditions (current app default)
